This work was developed as part of a Creative Europe–supported collaboration with Orbe and Coventry University’s Centre for Dance Research for Vibes#5, a participatory dance project designed for communal settings and audiences of different ages. The score was built as an interactive system rather than a fixed composition, allowing musical structure to respond to participants while remaining legible, accessible, and formally coherent.

The project explored how interactive music might support non-linear choreography without reducing the score to reaction or effect. I created a set of responsive musical structures that could accommodate movement, changing group formations, and varying levels of participant agency. The music was composed through an expert system I designed, using musical materials that remained clear and widely graspable in participatory settings, including polka, jig, contemporary classical minimalism, and New Orleans brass-band idioms.

A central concern of the work was accessibility. The score needed to support communal dance without requiring specialist musical knowledge from participants, while still offering enough structural variation and contrast to sustain interest and shape the experience over time. Rather than treating the audience as passive recipients or as isolated controllers, the project framed participation as something collective, negotiated, and musically consequential.

Vibes premiered at Birmingham International Dance Festival and was later presented at OperaEstate Festival in Bassano del Grappa. The work formed part of a broader line of research and production around interactive scores, communal dance, and systems that reconnect music with participation, bodily movement, and social coordination.

*Developed in collaboration with Orbe and Coventry University’s Centre for Dance Research. Choreographic collaborators included Karen Wood, Rosemary Lee, and Scott Delahunta.*
