Triadic Mutualism

2026-02-20 · mixed

Collective adaptive music and generative visuals for networked audiences.

mesomacromusicalvisualsocialbehavioralcontextcollective behaviorparticipatory systemadaptive musicgenerative visualsnetworked audienceecological metaphor

Triadic Mutualism is a networked participatory audiovisual environment in which collective movement shapes a shared musical and visual system across multiple sites. Participants enter a projected field where their gestures, rhythms, density, stillness, clustering, and coordination influence the evolving state of the work. Rather than triggering isolated effects, they act inside an emergent system whose local and global states are continuously negotiated through motion, proximity, exchange, and co-presence.

The work is structured as a bio-informed ecology. Activity, coordination, and interaction influence variables such as energy, diversity, migration, metabolic balance, and resource circulation. These in turn affect both the projected environment and the music it generates. Tempo, texture, intensity, and larger formal developments emerge from collective patterns rather than from a fixed score. Music is therefore treated not as a static composition but as a responsive temporal condition shaped by group behaviour.

A defining aspect of the project is its capacity to connect multiple sites in real time. Through designated portal zones within the projection, participants can engage with audiences in other cities, creating exchanges framed through concepts such as migration, flock health, resource transfer, and distributed consequence. Remote audiences appear not as literal representations but as abstract presences inside a shared rule system, making visible the relation between local action and remote effect.

The work sits at the intersection of participatory installation, embodied sonification, social choreography, and systems-based practice. It treats music as emergent system state and asks how collective behaviour can become compositional material. The project brings together collective behaviour sensing, social musical communication, ecological metaphor, socio-musicological and bio-informed modelling, rule-based generation, and multiagent logic. It explores how musical, visual, and social layers can be connected without losing clarity, and how authored structure can remain legible within a responsive environment.

Triadic Mutualism was developed under Telharmonium Labs, Inc. Documentation is still in progress.
