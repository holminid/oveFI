Biomechanical Duet

2025-07-01 · video

Music and dance were once the same thing.

micromesomusicalbehavioralgestureduetphysically modelled soundimprovisationperformance

Excerpt from a performance with Liliana Salazar and Ove Holmqvist at Chimerik’s Unity Consciousness 2025 festival at the Roundhouse in Vancouver.

Music and dance were once the same thing. How can we use technology to turn dancers into musicians and musicians into dancers?

In this improvisation, motion data from four Movesense sensors connected to miRack through the Holonist app. Liliana’s gestures voiced two physically modelled SWAM trumpets, while SWAM’s tenor trombone, played on a Roland Aerophone Mini over WIDI BLE MIDI through an iConnectivity A4+ interface, produced notes that interacted with the harmonic field set by the trumpets. The Eventide Qvox harmonizer was mapped to the angle of the horn, which created further brass accompaniments. A walking SWAM double bass was triggered by leg movement, and tempo was controlled by the shared average activity of all sensors, clocked via Ableton Link.

The result is not a sonification of dance but a distributed instrument in which movement, voicing, and structure are inseparable. Music and dance are once more united as one social instrument.
