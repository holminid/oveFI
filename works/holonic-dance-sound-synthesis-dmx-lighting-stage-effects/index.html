Holonic Systems: responsive music and performance environments

2019-01-01 · video

Heart-rate and movement/posture sonification control sound & lights.

Holonic Systems developed responsive performance and participatory music systems in which movement data shaped musical structure, synthesis behaviour, and, when relevant, DMX-controlled lighting and stage effects through a shared logic. The work brought sound, bodily movement, and visual atmosphere into the same compositional framework rather than treating them as separate production layers.

Across these projects, gesture, posture, and activity levels were used as compositional inputs rather than as triggers for isolated effects. Movement could influence musical parameters such as timbre, articulation, density, and structural development, while the same contextual model informed lighting changes and stage behaviour. This made it possible to maintain coherence across media without reducing the work to simple synchronisation or spectacle.

A key concern throughout this work was legibility. In systems that span sound, performance, and lighting, mapping logic can easily disappear behind the result. Holonic Systems therefore treated clear signal paths and coherent system behaviour as part of the artistic and technical method. This body of work formed a major part of the development of Holon and Holonist, and of a broader practice of building participatory and performance-oriented music systems in which adaptation remains authored, constrained, and intelligible.
